## Article I — Name and Purpose
1. Name: Fellowship for American Enterprise (the “Corporation”).
2. Purpose: The Corporation is organized and operated exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. The Corporation’s mission is to deliver proven digital economic infrastructure to communities by unifying local stakeholders behind systems that already work, modeled on results from Andrews, NC.
3. Prohibitions: No private inurement; no campaign intervention; limited lobbying consistent with law.

## Article II — Offices and Registered Agent
1. Principal Office: [TBD street, city, state, ZIP], North Carolina.
2. Registered Agent and Office: [TBD name and address], North Carolina.
3. Additional offices may be established by the Board.

## Article III — Board of Directors
1. Authority: The business and affairs of the Corporation are managed under the direction of the Board of Directors (“Board”).
2. Number: The Board shall consist of not fewer than 3 directors; the exact number is set by Board resolution.
3. Qualifications: Directors must support the mission and meet legal eligibility requirements.
4. Terms: Directors serve staggered terms of [TBD 1–3] years as set by resolution; directors serve until successors are elected and qualified.
5. Election: Directors are elected by the Board.
6. Removal/Vacancies: The Board may remove directors with or without cause as permitted by law; vacancies are filled by Board vote for the unexpired term.
7. Resignation: A director may resign by written notice to the Secretary.
8. Compensation: Directors serve without compensation except for reasonable expenses approved by the Board.

## Article IV — Meetings of the Board
1. Regular Meetings: Held at least quarterly on dates set by the Board.
2. Special Meetings: Called by the President/Chair, Secretary, or any two directors.
3. Notice: At least two days’ notice unless waived.
4. Quorum: A majority of directors in office constitutes a quorum.
5. Voting: Actions require a majority of directors present unless a greater vote is required by law or these Bylaws.
6. Participation: Meetings may be held by teleconference or videoconference if all participants can hear each other.
7. Consent in Writing: Actions may be taken without a meeting by unanimous written consent, including electronic signatures.
8. Recusal: Directors must recuse from matters where they have a conflict of interest per the Conflict of Interest Policy.

## Article V — Officers
1. Officers: President/Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, and any other officers the Board designates.
2. Election and Term: Officers are elected annually by the Board and serve until successors are elected and qualified.
3. Duties:
   - President/Chair: Presides over meetings; provides leadership and oversight of Board matters.
   - Secretary: Maintains minutes, records, notices, and corporate documents.
   - Treasurer: Oversees finances, budgets, and financial reports.
4. Removal/Vacancies: Officers may be removed by the Board; vacancies filled by Board vote for the unexpired term.

## Article VI — Committees
1. The Board may establish committees and delegate authority consistent with law. Committees report to the Board.
2. No committee may exercise authority to amend Articles or Bylaws, elect or remove directors, or approve dissolution.

## Article VII — Conflicts of Interest
1. The Corporation shall maintain and enforce a Conflict of Interest Policy adopted by the Board.
2. Directors and officers must disclose conflicts annually and as they arise; conflicted individuals abstain from related votes.
3. All recusals and determinations are recorded in the minutes.

## Article VIII — Indemnification and Liability
1. To the fullest extent permitted by North Carolina law, the Corporation shall indemnify directors, officers, employees, and agents.
2. The liability of directors is limited as allowed by the North Carolina Nonprofit Corporation Act.

## Article IX — Fiscal Matters
1. Fiscal Year: The fiscal year shall end on [TBD — e.g., December 31] unless changed by Board resolution.
2. Checks and Disbursements: Authorized signatories are set by Board resolution; dual controls and segregation of duties are encouraged.
3. Records and Reports: The Treasurer ensures accurate books and timely reports to the Board.

## Article X — Records and Transparency
1. The Corporation shall maintain accurate books, minutes, and governing documents. Official copies are stored in the repository per the governance constitution.
2. Minutes will document attendance, quorum, motions, votes, recusals, and resolutions.
3. Annual reviews of governance documents and policies shall be scheduled and recorded.

## Article XI — Non-Discrimination
The Corporation shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any category protected by law in any of its policies, programs, or activities.

## Article XII — Amendments
These Bylaws may be amended by a majority vote of all directors then in office, provided notice of the proposed amendment is given in the meeting notice or waived by all directors.

## Article XIII — Dissolution
On dissolution, assets shall be distributed as set forth in the Articles of Incorporation to one or more organizations described in Section 501(c)(3) or to a federal, state, or local government for public purposes.
